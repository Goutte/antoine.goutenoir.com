    MÃ», a board game with five elements

    Posted on 2023, Nov 06 2 mins read

    Available in HTML5

    Godot being amazing, I exported an HTML5 build of the game that you can run in your web browser.

    Play the HTML5 Demo

    The Rules of MÃ»

    You win by achieving any of the following:

    • Reach the top of the opponent’s hill.
    • Capture both the opponent’s priests.
    • Capture all the opponent’s pawns.

    Movement

    Pawns and priests move alike, to one of the three tiles in front of them, if it is on the board and not already occupied by another pawn.

    When reaching the last line, pawns and priests may move laterally towards the top of the hill.

    Capture

    Just like checkers, except you can sacrifice your pawn and jump outside the board.

    Capture is mandatory.

    If multiple capture solutions are available, you may freely choose which will proceed.

    Sacrifice

    Sacrifice is never mandatory.

    Sacrifice is not allowed if other capture solutions are available.

    Priests may not sacrifice themselves, even if it’s worth it.

    Priests, like Gods, die when have no more believers : a priest without any pawn of the same element is removed automatically.

    Teleportation

    But wait, there’s a game-changing twist !

    Whenever a pawn reaches the last line, the priest of the color of the pawn is teleported to the position of the pawn. The pawn is teleported to the position of the priest, if it was on the board.

    This happens even during a capture chain, and even if the priest was captured previously.

    The Game

    I made this game to humour my dad and play the game with him.

    Thanks to Philippe Lheureux for crafting this interesting game and letting me make a video game version of it !

    The Jam

    This was a quick jam, so the game lacks polish, and a real AI. Perhaps I’ll pick it up someday during another game jam ?
